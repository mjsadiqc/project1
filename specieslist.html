ENDANGERED SPECIES

Sumatran Orangutan (Pongo abelii):

The Sumatran Orangutan is one of the three orangutan species and is native to the island of Sumatra in Indonesia. It is critically endangered due to habitat loss caused by deforestation for agriculture and logging. Orangutans are highly intelligent and mainly arboreal, depending on the rainforest for their survival.

Amur Leopard (Panthera pardus orientalis):

The Amur Leopard is a critically endangered big cat species found in the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It is one of the rarest and most endangered feline species in the world, with less than 100 individuals estimated to exist in the wild. Habitat loss, poaching, and illegal wildlife trade pose significant threats to their survival.

Hawksbill Sea Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata):

The Hawksbill Sea Turtle is a critically endangered marine turtle species. It inhabits coral reefs in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide. Hawksbill turtles are known for their beautifully patterned shells. They face threats such as habitat degradation, climate change, pollution, and poaching for their shells, which are used in the illegal trade.

Sumatran Tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae):

The Sumatran Tiger is a critically endangered subspecies of tiger native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra. It is one of the smallest tiger subspecies and faces numerous threats, including habitat loss, illegal hunting, and poaching for the illegal wildlife trade. Conservation efforts aim to protect its remaining habitat and combat poaching.

Vaquita Porpoise (Phocoena sinus):

The Vaquita Porpoise is a critically endangered marine mammal found in the Gulf of California, Mexico. It is the world's most endangered cetacean, with an estimated population of fewer than 10 individuals. Vaquitas are often caught as bycatch in gillnets used for illegal fishing activities, leading to their rapid decline.
